Solar Street Light Installation Guide: Step-by-Step for Commercial Projects

Expert insights from Minglu Lighting — your solar and LED lighting partner

2026-06-24·10 min·Installation Guide

Proper installation is the single biggest factor determining solar street light performance and lifespan. Even the highest-quality light will fail prematurely if installed incorrectly. This guide walks through every step — from site survey to final testing.

Pre-Installation: Site Survey

Before ordering lights, conduct a thorough site survey:

  • Solar exposure — Measure daily sun hours. Solar panels need 4-6+ hours of direct sunlight. Use a solar pathfinder or mobile app to map shading from buildings, trees, and poles.
  • Pole spacing — Calculate based on light pole height (usually 3-4x pole height between poles for uniform illumination)
  • Ground conditions — Check soil type (clay, sand, rock) for foundation design. Rocky soil may need smaller foundations; sandy soil needs deeper ones.
  • Cable routing — For split-type systems, plan the underground cable path between solar panel pole and light pole

Critical tip: Use a template/jig to hold anchor bolts in the correct position during concrete pour. Misaligned bolts are the #1 installation error.

Step 2: Grounding and Lightning Protection

Steel poles act as lightning rods. Install a proper grounding system:

  • Drive a 1.5m+ copper grounding rod at each pole base
  • Connect the rod to the pole with 10mm² copper wire
  • Ground resistance should be < 10Ω (test with a ground resistance tester)
  • For areas with frequent lightning, add a surge protection device (SPD) in the controller compartment

Step 3: Light Assembly

All-in-One Solar Street Lights

The simplest installation — the solar panel, battery, LED, and controller are integrated into one unit:

  1. Unpack and inspect for shipping damage
  2. Attach the mounting bracket to the light housing (4-6 bolts)
  3. Mount the bracket onto the pole top (clamp or bolt-on)
  4. Adjust the angle: solar panel should face south (Northern Hemisphere) at an angle equal to latitude + 10°
  5. Torque all bolts to specification (typically 40-60 N·m for M16)

Split-Type Systems

Solar panel and light fixture are separate, connected by cable:

  1. Mount the solar panel bracket at the top of the pole, oriented for maximum sun exposure
  2. Mount the light fixture arm at the desired height (6-10m typical)
  3. Route the solar cable through the pole (internal) or along the outside (conduit-recommended)
  4. Connect to controller in the battery compartment (usually at pole base)
  5. Waterproof all connections with heat-shrink tubing and silicone sealant

Step 4: Battery Installation

For split-type systems, batteries may be buried underground (gel batteries) or mounted in a weatherproof box at the pole base (lithium):

  • Underground battery box — Dig a hole 300mm away from the pole, install waterproof battery box, backfill with sand for drainage
  • Pole-mounted battery — Installed in a lockable compartment at 1.5-2m height for anti-theft
  • All battery terminals must have anti-corrosion grease applied
  • Connect temperature sensor (if provided) for lithium batteries — prevents charging in freezing conditions

Step 5: Controller Configuration

Before powering on, configure the MPPT/PWM controller:

  • Set battery type (LiFePO4 / Gel / Lead-Acid)
  • Set lighting schedule (dusk-to-dawn / timer mode / motion sensor mode)
  • Set dimming profile (e.g., 100% for first 4 hours, 50% for remaining)
  • Set low-voltage disconnect (LVD) — typically 11.5V for 12V systems
  • Test all modes before finalizing

Common Installation Mistakes

  • Wrong panel orientation — Even 15° off optimal can reduce output by 10-15%
  • Shadow from adjacent poles — Check for mutual shading between closely-spaced lights
  • Insufficient concrete curing — Raising poles before concrete reaches 70% strength causes leaning
  • Loose connections — Vibration from wind loosens terminals over time; use spring washers
  • No weatherproofing on cable entries — Water ingress kills controllers within months
