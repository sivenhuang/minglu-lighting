Top Solar Street Light Manufacturers in China 2026

A B2B buyer's guide to the best solar street light factories in Guangdong, Zhejiang, and beyond

June 24, 2026 · 9 min read · Industry Research

If you're sourcing solar street lights for a commercial or municipal project, China is the world's largest manufacturing hub. Guangdong province alone — where Minglu Lighting is headquartered — produces over 60% of the world's solar outdoor lighting products. But not all manufacturers are equal.

This guide helps you identify quality suppliers, understand what to look for, and avoid common sourcing pitfalls.

Why Source Solar Street Lights from China?

China's solar lighting industry has matured rapidly over the last decade. Advantages include:

  • Cost advantage — 30-50% lower than Western manufacturers for equivalent quality
  • Complete supply chain — solar panels, lithium batteries, LED chips, and aluminum housings are all produced domestically
  • Scale — Chinese factories routinely handle orders from 100 to 10,000+ units
  • Certifications — leading manufacturers hold ISO 9001, CE, RoHS, and often IEC or SASO certifications
  • R&D investment — top-tier factories now offer integrated all-in-one designs with MPPT controllers and IoT monitoring

How to Evaluate a Solar Street Light Manufacturer

1. Certifications

Minimum requirements for international buyers:

  • ISO 9001 — quality management system
  • CE — required for European market entry
  • RoHS — restriction of hazardous substances
  • IEC 61215 / IEC 61730 — solar panel testing standards
  • SASO — required for Saudi Arabia and GCC markets

2. Production Capacity

Ask about monthly output, lead times, and minimum order quantities (MOQ). A medium-sized factory should handle 5,000-10,000 units per month with lead times of 15-30 days. Small MOQs (10-50 units) are common for sample orders.

3. In-House vs Outsourced Components

The best manufacturers produce key components in-house — notably the aluminum housing and assembly. This gives better quality control and faster delivery. Battery sourcing is usually external (CATL, BYD, or tier-2 lithium). LED chips typically come from Bridgelux, Philips, or Osram.

4. Export Experience

A manufacturer with 5+ years of export experience will understand documentation (Certificate of Origin, Form A, Form E), logistics (FOB/CIF/DDP), and regional compliance requirements. Ask about their top export markets.

5. After-Sales Support

Warranty periods of 2-5 years are standard. Check if they stock spare parts, provide remote technical support, and have an English-speaking sales team.

Red Flags When Sourcing

  • Prices too good to be true — sub-$50 solar street lights likely use recycled batteries or fake wattage ratings
  • No factory visit allowed — could be a trading company posing as a factory
  • No certifications — non-negotiable for most international projects
  • Poor communication — if pre-sale communication is slow, post-sale support will be worse
