How to Choose Solar Street Lights for Your Project

Complete buying guide for commercial and municipal solar street lighting

Published: June 22, 2026 8 min read By Minglu Lighting

Solar street lights have become the go-to solution for commercial, municipal, and industrial lighting projects worldwide. They eliminate trenching costs, reduce electricity bills to zero, and operate off-grid. But choosing the wrong specifications can lead to dim lighting, short battery life, and wasted investment.

This guide walks you through every factor you need to consider when selecting solar street lights for your project — from wattage and battery capacity to IP ratings and pole height.

1. Determine Your Lighting Requirements

Before looking at products, define what you need the lights to accomplish:

  • Roadway lighting: Typically requires 60W–200W LED output, 8–12 meter pole height
  • Parking lot lighting: 40W–120W, 6–8 meter poles
  • Pathway / park lighting: 20W–60W, 4–6 meter poles
  • Perimeter / security lighting: 60W–150W with motion sensors

2. Understand Solar Panel Wattage vs. LED Wattage

A common mistake is confusing solar panel wattage with LED wattage. They are completely different:

  • LED wattage determines how bright the light is. A 60W LED produces roughly 6,000–9,000 lumens depending on efficiency.
  • Solar panel wattage determines how fast the battery recharges. For a 60W LED, you typically need a 100W–180W solar panel to ensure full charge in 4–6 hours of sunlight.
The solar panel wattage should be 1.5–3x the LED wattage to ensure reliable year-round operation, especially in regions with shorter winter days.

3. Battery Capacity: The Most Critical Spec

Battery capacity determines how many nights the light can run without recharging. This is measured in Watt-hours (Wh) or Amp-hours (Ah) at the system voltage.

Battery Types Compared

  • LiFePO4 (Lithium Iron Phosphate): 2,000+ cycles, safe, performs well in heat. Best choice for commercial projects.
  • Ternary Lithium (Li-NMC): Higher energy density, 1,200+ cycles. Good for compact designs.
  • Lead-Acid (GEL): 300–500 cycles, cheap but heavy. Not recommended for new installations.

For reliable operation, the battery should store enough energy for 2–3 nights of autonomy (no sunlight days). For example, a 60W LED running 12 hours/night needs approximately 720Wh per night. A 2-night autonomy requires at least 1,440Wh battery capacity.

4. LED Lumens and Light Distribution

Wattage alone doesn't tell the full story. Two 60W LEDs can produce very different brightness depending on LED chip efficiency (measured in lm/W):

  • Standard chips: 100–130 lm/W → 60W = 6,000–7,800 lumens
  • High-efficiency chips: 150–180 lm/W → 60W = 9,000–10,800 lumens

Also consider the beam angle and light distribution pattern. For roadways, a Type III or Type V distribution with 120°–150° beam angle covers the road evenly.

5. IP Rating and Durability

Outdoor solar lights must withstand rain, dust, and extreme temperatures. Look for:

  • IP65: Protected against water jets — minimum acceptable for outdoor use
  • IP66: Protected against powerful water jets — recommended for coastal or heavy-rain areas
  • IP67: Temporary immersion — best for flood-prone regions

Also check the operating temperature range. Quality lights should work from -30°C to +65°C.

6. Smart Controllers and Motion Sensors

Modern solar street lights include intelligent controllers that extend battery life:

  • Time-based dimming: Full brightness for first 4 hours, 50% brightness after midnight
  • Motion sensors (PIR/Microwave): 30% standby brightness, 100% when motion detected — extends battery life by 40–60%
  • Remote monitoring: Some models offer app-based control via GSM/LoRa for large-scale projects

7. Pole Height and Spacing

Correct pole height and spacing ensure uniform illumination without dark spots:

Application Pole Height Spacing Recommended LED
Residential road6–8m25–35m30–60W
Main road8–12m30–45m80–150W
Highway12–15m40–60m150–300W
Parking lot6–8m20–30m40–80W

8. Certifications to Look For

For commercial and government projects, certifications matter:

  • CE: Required for European market
  • RoHS: Restriction of hazardous substances
  • ISO 9001: Quality management system
  • IP65/IP66: Ingress protection rating
  • IEC 61215 / IEC 61730: Solar panel quality and safety

Conclusion

Choosing the right solar street light comes down to matching specifications to your project's needs. The key checklist:

  1. Match LED wattage to your area size and pole height
  2. Ensure solar panel is 1.5–3x the LED wattage
  3. Choose LiFePO4 battery with 2–3 nights autonomy
  4. Verify IP65+ rating for your climate
  5. Include smart controller with motion sensor for energy savings
  6. Check certifications (CE, RoHS, ISO 9001)
